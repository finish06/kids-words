## What Worked

- **Audit-before-build recovery pattern** (twice). When resuming a stale cycle, scanning the actual codebase first turns out to be the highest-leverage move. Cycle-8 found 5 of 6 items done; cycle-9 found items 1+2 entirely shipped via PR #7. Roughly 6+ hours of duplicate work avoided.
- **Pragmatic merging when CI is mis-wired.** Once the default-branch / PR-base mismatch was diagnosed, the right call was to merge based on local tests and let post-merge CI on main confirm. That unblocked the session in minutes.
- **End-to-end staging smoke test via cmux browser.** Real UI verification with the Guest profile through a complete 5-word round closed the loop on cycle-9 in a way that curl alone couldn't.

## What Didn't

- **CI debugging loop got too long.** I burned 30+ minutes diagnosing why CI wasn't firing before realizing the PRs were targeting `feat/word-image-matching` instead of `main`. The user signaled overwhelm ("I am so lost on what we need to do next") before I identified the real cause. Lesson: sooner pivot to "give one next step + investigate in parallel."
- **Spec metadata is becoming aspirational rather than descriptive.** 13 of 15 specs still say `Draft` despite many being shipped. The spec is no longer a reliable source of truth on what's done.
- **Learnings live in markdown, not JSON.** Smart filtering by stack/category isn't possible until migrated.
- **6 milestones IN_PROGRESS** is cognitive debt. The hill chart is harder to read than it should be.
